**EXECUTIVE BRIEF FOR UPLOAD**

1. **OVERALL PICTURE**
   Eight individuals testified on Senate Bill 602, with six in favor, two in favor with amendments, and one providing informational testimony. Key stakeholder groups included mental health organizations, legal professionals, and parental advocacy groups. The core issue centers around establishing an Algorithmic Addiction Fund to address mental health challenges caused by algorithm-driven social media addictions, particularly among youth.

2. **KEY VOICES**
   - **Andrea Mansfield, Licensed Clinical Professional Counselors of Maryland:** Strongly supports the bill, emphasizing the harmful effects of social media on youth mental health and the need for a dedicated fund to address these issues.
   - **Ashley Clark, Maryland Psychiatric Society:** Argues that algorithmic addiction is a significant public health issue, advocating for the bill to fund statewide assessments and interventions.
   - **Christine McComas, Parental Advocacy (Parents SOS):** Shares a personal story of loss due to social media addiction, urging for comprehensive measures to counteract online harms and cyberbullying.
   - **Katie Fry Hester, Maryland State Senator:** Supports the bill, highlighting the deliberate addictive design of social media platforms and proposing the fund as a public health response akin to the Opioid Restitution Fund.
   - **Ann Geddes, Mental Health Association of Maryland:** Backs the bill with an amendment to allocate unspent funds to school-based behavioral health services, addressing the mental health crisis among youth.

3. **WATCH POINTS**
   - **Amendments and Allocation**: Note proposed amendments that suggest unspent funds be directed toward school-based behavioral health services, creating a sustainable funding stream, and align related proposed legislation.
   - **Absence of Proven Treatments**: The Maryland Department of Health pointed out the lack of established evidence-based treatments for algorithmic addiction, which might pose challenges in implementing the fund’s objectives.
   - **Legal and Financial Implications**: Consider alignment with concurrent proposals like the Attorney General Enforcement Recovery Fund, which could impact the allocation and administration of collected penalties and settlements.
